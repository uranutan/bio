  6. (Survey Data) Phishing and Cybercrime Risks in a University Student Community.

    Date: mid-year 2018

    in collaboration with Prof. R. Broadhurst at the ANU Cybercrime Observatory

    In an exploratory quasi-experimental observational study, 138 participants recruited during a university orientation week were exposed to social engineering directives in the form of fake email or phishing attacks over several months in 2017. These email attacks attempted to elicit personal information from participants, or entice them into clicking links which may have been compromised in a real-world setting. The study aimed to determine the risks of cybercrime for students by observing their responses to social engineering and exploring attitudes to cybercrime risks before and after the phishing phase. See the publication R. Broadhurst, K. Skinner, N. Sifniotis, B. Matamoros-Macias, and Y. Ipsen, International Journal of Cybersecurity Intelligence and Cybercrime, vol. 2, no. 1, pp. 4-23pp., http://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.3176319

    R Cybercrime Discrete Data Hypothesis Testing

  8. (Insurance Model) The effects of largest claim and excess of loss reinsurance on a company’s ruin time and valuation

    Date: 2016

    in collaboration with Prof P. Griffin at Syracuse University and Prof. Szimayer, A of Universität Hamburg, both are experts in insurance economic models with stochastic processes

    We compare two types of reinsurance: excess of loss (EOL) and largest claim reinsurance (LCR) and documented the primary insurer’s point of view in terms of assessment of risk and payment of reinsurance premium. Assuming the classical compound Poisson risk model with choices of claim size distributions (classified as heavy, medium and light-tailed cases), simulations are used to illustrate the impact of the EOL and LCR treaties on the company’s ruin probability, ruin time and value as determined by the dividend discounting model. See the publication Fan, Y., Wang, T., Griffin, P., Maller, R. A. and Szimayer, A., Risks. 5, 3, 2017. , DOI:10.3390/risks5010003.

    R Simulation Reinsurance Model Poisson Process
